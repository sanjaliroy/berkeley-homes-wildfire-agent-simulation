Week 14: Project Report (20%)
The final report should clearly document your GenAI system, why you built it this way, and how well it works.

Step 1: Create and submit a PDF document (at most 5 pages long, excluding the optional appendix) that includes:
Full names of all team members.
Berkeley.edu email addresses for each member.
Project report covering: 
Abstract (1 paragraph): Summarize the problem you are addressing, the type of GenAI system you built (e.g., pre-trained LLM, fine-tuned LLM, RAG system, agentic workflow), and the most important finding from your evaluation.
Introduction (0.5 pages): Define the problem and motivate your approach. Explain why a GenAI approach is appropriate. Clearly define the inputs and outputs of your system: "Our system takes {specific input type} and uses {specific GenAI techniques/models} to produce {specific output}." Briefly outline the structure of the reminder of the report.
Related work (0.5 pages): Review at least two relevant papers, systems, or products. Compare their approaches to yours and explain how your system builds upon, differs from, or improves on existing work.
Data sources and inputs (0.5 page): Describe in detail your data sources and inputs. See the milestone section for concrete examples.
System architecture and variants (1 page): Clearly describe the overall system architecture and variants you have implemented for your GenAI system. See the milestone section for concrete examples
Experiments and evaluation (2 pages): This is one of the most important sections of your report. See the milestone section for concrete examples.
Discussion (0.25 pages). Reflect on your findings:
Which system variants had the largest impact on performance, and what evidence supports your explanation?
Which system variant failed most noticeably, and under what conditions?
Did you observe systematic failure modes or biases across system variants?
What trade-offs did you observe between output quality, cost, latency, and overall system complexity across system variants?
How robust and generalizable was your best-performing system variant to new, noisy, or out-of-distribution inputs?
Conclusions and future work (0.25 pages). Summarize your key contributions and findings:
What are the most important insights from your work?
What limitations or constraints influenced your results?
What would you most want to improve or extend with more time, data, or compute?
Contributions: Provide a detailed breakdown of each team member's specific contributions to both the writing and coding components of the project. I am okay if some notebooks are collaborative, as long as each team member has at least one notebook where they are the main contributor. Each team member should contribute to the System architecture and variants and Experiments and evaluation sections. This documentation ensures fair workload distribution and helps assess individual contributions.
Appendix (optional): Include additional details that support your work but aren't essential to the main report, such as additional example outputs, detailed hyperparameter search results, prompt templates used, cost and latency analysis.

Step 2: Submit one report per team to Gradescope. Please make sure to tag all the team members in your submission.

Guidelines for PDF submissions: You can use either single-column or two-column layouts. Paper size should be Letter (8.5 x 11 inches) with font size between 10-12 points and margins of at least 0.5 inch on all sides. Your submission must be in PDF format.
